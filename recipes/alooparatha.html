Aloo Paratha is a popular and hearty Indian flatbread stuffed with a flavorful, spiced mashed potato filling. It's often enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner and is typically served hot with a dollop of butter, plain yogurt (curd), or a side of pickle (achaar) and chutneys. The key to a perfect paratha is a soft outer layer and a well-seasoned, evenly spread filling.

Ingredients List

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp oil (optional)
  • Water, as needed for kneading
  • 3 large potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped red onion (optional)
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped fresh coriander leaves
  • 1 tsp ground coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried mango powder (amchur) or lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Ghee or oil, for cooking the parathas
  • Butter, plain yogurt, and pickles/chutneys, for serving

Steps to Make

  1. Prepare the Dough: Mix flour, salt, and water to knead a soft dough. Let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling: Combine mashed potatoes with all the specified filling ingredients and mix thoroughly.
  3. Assemble the Paratha: Divide both the dough and the filling into equal-sized balls.
  4. Stuff the Dough: Flatten a dough ball, place a filling ball inside, pinch the edges to seal, and flatten gently.
  5. Roll the Paratha: Lightly dust with flour and gently roll the stuffed ball into a 6-7 inch circle.
  6. Cook the Paratha: Heat a flat griddle (tava) over medium-high heat. Place the paratha on the hot griddle.
  7. Apply Ghee/Oil: Flip the paratha once bubbles appear. Brush ghee or oil on both sides.
  8. Press and Cook: Gently press the paratha with a spatula until golden brown spots appear and it puffs up slightly.
  9. Serve: Serve immediately with butter, yogurt, and your choice of pickle/chutney.
